The iOS Security Gap

When a sophisticated threat hits your iOS fleet, you're in the dark. No runtime monitoring. No behavioral analysis. No incident response capability. Just hope — and that's not a security strategy.

Traditional endpoint security stops at the desktop. iOS and macOS devices account for over 25% of mobile endpoints across U.S. federal agencies, yet no incumbent vendor provides real-time, on-device endpoint detection within Apple's strict sandbox. Detection happens after the breach, not before.

Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Two categories of tools claim to protect iOS. Neither actually does.

Mobile Device Management

MDM tools manage device configuration, enforce policies, and distribute apps. They were never designed for threat detection.

  • No runtime monitoring or behavioral analysis
  • Cannot detect malware, phishing, or zero-day exploits
  • No incident response capability

Device management is necessary. But it's not security.

Cloud-Dependent EDR

Traditional EDR vendors must exfiltrate device data to the cloud for analysis. On iOS, Apple's sandbox makes this fundamentally limited.

  • Cannot operate within Apple's sandbox constraints
  • Fails in air-gapped and disconnected environments
  • Cloud offloading violates zero-trust principles

Detection after the breach isn't detection. It's damage assessment.

Lunar Defense operates on-device, inside the sandbox, with zero cloud dependency.
